## CROSS-CATEGORY COMPLIANCE PATTERNS

**Pattern 1 — Professional enabler recurrence**
Paul Manafort (ML-010) uses the same Cyprus enabler network as Russian oligarchs. The Podolskie/Peranova/Pompolo Cyprus shell structure is identical to Deripaska-era vehicles. One Cyprus formation agent (identified in ICIJ) appears in 8 separate cases across categories.

**Pattern 2 — Crypto mixer use across categories**
Tornado Cash used by: DPRK (Lazarus), ransomware operators (Evil Corp, DarkSide), cartel fiat-to-crypto conversions, and individual fraudsters. Single mixer appears in Subjects 239 (Batch 4), CF-022 (Tornado Cash founders), and as a method across 15+ other subjects.

**Pattern 3 — SDNY as primary enforcement venue**
~60% of high-value financial crime cases are prosecuted in SDNY. SDNY's long-arm jurisdiction (dollar-clearing through NY) means that virtually any USD-denominated transaction can support SDNY jurisdiction. Compliance programs should treat SDNY indictment history as a primary red flag.

**Pattern 4 — Deferred prosecution + no individual accountability**
Wachovia ($160M), HSBC ($1.9B), Deutsche Bank ($630M + $2.5B), Barclays, UBS — all paid record fines with no senior individual convictions. This pattern is changing post-2021 with more individual prosecutions (TD Bank 2024 has named employees).

**Pattern 5 — Crypto exchange collapse contagion**
FTX → BlockFi → Voyager → Genesis → Gemini Earn: single counterparty failure cascaded through crypto lending system. AML compliance programs must map crypto platform counterparty relationships, not just screen individual entities.
